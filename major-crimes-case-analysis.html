Evidence Items

What Was Examined

The investigation covered six distinct evidence sources across three suspects: Orion (Christopher Austin), Andromeda, and Cassiopeia. Evidence ranged from a physical laptop image to cloud accounts, Facebook, Instagram, and a Kingston SD card.

The username "Orion" is forensically significant — it is a known alias for suspect Christopher Austin, establishing a direct link between the device and the subject of the investigation.

Key Findings

What the Evidence Showed

  • Browser Cookies Both Firefox and Edge contained cookies tied to gun-related websites, indicating sustained interest in firearms acquisition.
  • Chrome Downloads 34 files downloaded via Chrome, including Notre Dame Stadium.jpg — matching a location of interest in the case.
  • Silk Road Visit Subject directly typed http://www.silkroad.com/ into Edge on 8/27/2020 at 7:35 PM, evidencing dark web activity.
  • Google Maps Queries Searches for Notre Dame Stadium and Kodak Firing Range — two locations tied to the training and the alleged planned incident.
  • Andromeda Emails 29 emails received from Andromeda G., including image attachments showing firearms, ammunition, shooting targets, and training photos. One image was also found locally on the hard drive under the renamed filename Training06.jpg.
  • Files Sent to Andromeda Orion emailed Andromeda 6 files including a robbery data Excel sheet, 2 PDFs, and image files — establishing conspiracy communication.
  • Social Media Orion's Facebook tied to orionC2290@gmail.com (574-302-5282). Cassiopeia identified as Facebook user "Cassiopeia Kov" — no direct messages with Orion found beyond a Google Cloud contact entry.
  • SD Card Match Andromeda's Kingston SD card contained four photos identical (same hash) to images tagged on the HP laptop: IMG_0055.HEIC, IMG_0051.HEIC, IMG_0043.HEIC, IMG_0025.HEIC.
  • Training Trip On 7/17/2020, Orion and Andromeda traveled from Courtney Lane, South Bend, Indiana to a training location off Chippewa Trail at Lake Skegemog — corroborated by GPS data in the Google Takeout evidence.
Methodology

How the Investigation Was Conducted

All analysis was performed in Magnet AXIOM. Key techniques included filtered keyword searches (gun, silkroad, Notre Dame, Cassiopeia, meet), artifact navigation across browser history, cookies, emails, downloads, Google Maps queries, and cloud data, and image comparison using AXIOM's similar-picture matching feature to identify renamed files.

Evidence tags were created throughout to document findings and support chain of custody: Firefox Gun Site Cookies, Edge Gun Site Cookies, Silkroad Visited, Google Map Queries, Andromeda emails, Andromeda gun images, Training06.jpg Image, and Cassiopeia Facebook.
