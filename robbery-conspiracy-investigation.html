Investigation Findings

Key Evidence Uncovered

  • OS & Identity Windows 10 Pro installed 6/24/2020. User account "Orion" — a confirmed alias for Christopher Austin. Timezone: US Eastern Standard Time.
  • Firearms Interest Both Firefox and Edge contained cookies from gun-related websites. 34 Chrome downloads included Notre Dame Stadium.jpg. Google Maps queries showed Notre Dame Stadium and Kodak Firing Range.
  • Dark Web Activity Orion directly typed silkroad.com into Edge on 8/27/2020, establishing intent to access a dark web marketplace.
  • Conspiracy Communication 29 emails received from Andromeda, including images of firearms, ammunition, and shooting targets. Orion sent Andromeda 6 files in return: a robbery data spreadsheet, 2 PDFs, and image files.
  • Photo Evidence An emailed photo was found on the local hard drive under the renamed filename Training06.jpg — identified via AXIOM's similar-picture matching feature. Four SD card images were hash-identical to laptop images: IMG_0055, IMG_0051, IMG_0043, IMG_0025 (.HEIC).
  • Social Media Network Orion tied to orionC2290@gmail.com / 574-302-5282 on Facebook. Cassiopeia identified as Facebook user "Cassiopeia Kov" — no direct messages with Orion, only a Google Cloud contact entry.
  • First Contact Chris and Alex (Orion and Andromeda) first met at the Chicory Café — found in the Communication artifact under a keyword search for "meet."
  • Training Trip GPS On 7/17/2020, the subjects traveled from Courtney Lane, South Bend, Indiana to a training location off Chippewa Trail at Lake Skegemog — corroborated by location data in the Google Takeout evidence with a date filter applied.
Investigative Approach

Methodology

The investigation was conducted entirely within Magnet AXIOM, working across all ten evidence items simultaneously. Artifacts examined included browser history, cookies, typed URLs, Chrome downloads, Outlook emails, email attachments, Google Maps queries, social media accounts, cloud passwords, and GPS location data.

Corroborating evidence across multiple sources was the key challenge: matching a renamed file on the laptop to an emailed attachment required AXIOM's image similarity feature, not just filename or hash matching — demonstrating how file renaming is insufficient to hide digital evidence.

Date filters in AXIOM were used to isolate activity on specific days, narrowing GPS data to reveal the training trip origin and destination with precision. All notable findings were tagged for chain of custody documentation.
